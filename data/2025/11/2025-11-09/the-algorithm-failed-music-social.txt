Terrence O’Brien’s latest Stepback column explores how recommendation engines dragged music discovery into a rut. As major platforms leaned on engagement metrics, playlists blurred into safe background noise, burying the human curators and local scenes that once surfaced risks. The column argues for rebuilding taste through newsletters, niche radio, record stores, and deliberate deep listening habits. If your queue feels numb, it might be time to step outside the algorithm’s loop and rediscover how messy, opinionated people share songs.
